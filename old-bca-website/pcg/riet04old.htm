Rietveld Workshop for Beginners

hosted by the School of Crystallography, Birkbeck College, University of London

15th - 16th April 2004

Course Sponsored by: PCG/SCMP-IoP, CCP14, & CMSD

Course Details

Following the success of the short Rietveld course held in conjunction with the BCA Spring Meeting at the University of Nottingham, 2002, the Physical Crystallography Group will hold another 2 day course aimed at those requiring training in the Rietveld refinement of crystal structures from powder diffraction data. The course is being organised by Dr Jeremy K. Cockcroft (Birkbeck) and Dr John S. O. Evans (Durham) and will be held in the School of Crystallography, Birkbeck College, London.

The course is scheduled for Thursday 15th and Friday 16th of April. The course will run from 11:00 to 19:00 (or later) on the Thursday and from 9:00 to 16:30 on the Friday so that people can depart in reasonable time for the weekend.

This workshop is aimed at beginners, e.g. new PhD students, and is not for those with significant experience in powder diffraction. As before, the course will be a mixture of lectures providing the basic necessary background to the method plus practical hands-on sessions on PCs. Given the size of the PC room in the School of Crystallography, Birkbeck College, numbers for the workshop will be strictly limited. Early registration is encouraged: in the event of over-demand, it may be necessary to limit the number of applicants from the same laboratory and research group.

The lectures will cover key aspects of Rietveld refinements with reference to data collected using laboratory X-ray, synchrotron X-ray, reactor and pulsed neutron sources. In addition, some aspects of basic crystallography necessary for the successful refinement of crystal structures from powder diffraction will be given. It is hoped that students will have had some prior exposure to symmetry, for example, by attending the symmetry lectures held at the BCA Spring Meeting at Manchester this year.

Registration Fees & Bursaries

There are no registration fees for this workshop for students or anyone else who is a paid-up member of the BCA. For non-members, the registration fee payable on the day will be £50. (If you are not a member, why not consider joining the BCA? It costs only £15 per annum.) In addition, the course has financial support from: (a) the Structural Condensed Matter Group (SCMP) of the IoP, (b) Collaborative Computing Project No. 14 (CCP14) for Single-Crystal and Powder Diffraction, and (c), from the Centre for Molecular Structure and Dynamics (CMSD). Bona fida PhD students on the course will be able to claim up to £100 towards accommodation and travel expenses on the production of valid expenditure receipts, while other BCA members (e.g. PDRAs) will receive support up to £70.

Accommodation & Subsistence

No accommodation is provided, though a list of a few hotels in the Bloomsbury area was recently provided by Dave Taylor on the BCA-IG web site for the BCA-IG autumn meeting in November 2003. (This link also included maps.) In addition, some students may choose to stay outside London and to commute in each day.

Reasonably prices lunches are available in the local canteens of various sections of the university (e.g. Senate House). More details will be provided on the day.
