From the Sept 98 issue of 'Crystallography News'

Physical Crystallography Group News

Co-opted Members of Physical Crystallography Group Committee 1998-1999

I am pleased to report that Dr. Sue Kilcoyne of University of St. Andrews, Department of Physics & Astronomy, has agreed to join the PCG Committee with effect from October 1998 as a co-opted member. A second nominee for co-opted membership has been approached and we are awaiting his reply.

Philips Prize for Physical Crystallography 1999

This is the time of year when we usually make a call for nominations for the Philips prize for Physical Crystallography. However, with the absence of a BCA Spring Meeting in 1999, the Philips prize will be awarded at the International Union of Crystallography Congress in Glasgow in August. This will be a good opportunity to showcase talent in Physical Crystallography (or Structural Physics - see below!) to the whole crystallographic community worldwide. Therefore, we urge you to start thinking now about possible nominees in the usual category of a youngish up-and-coming scientist with publications of note, whose career stands to benefit from the award of such a prize. Note that the nominations need not be restricted to UK-based scientists and perhaps, in the year of IUCr, we could reasonably appoint an overseas winner (since such a person might well be attending IUCr anyway). The usual formal call for nominations and the pro-forma will appear in December's issue of Crystallography News.

Name Change from Physical Crystallography Group of the IOP to Structural Physics Group

The process of changing our identity from PCG to Structural Physics is underway. The next step is that the proposal be taken to the Condensed Matter and Materials Physics (CMMP) divisional meeting, which will take place some time in September. Our representative at the divisional meeting is Dr. Malcolm McMahon of Liverpool University. No problems with the name-change are envisaged, but one of the other groups may come up with some objection at the meeting, so we will have to wait and see what transpires....

Autumn Meeting:
Structural Physics of Functional Materials, University of Warwick, 25/11/98

The overall aim of the meeting is to demonstrate how crystallography, structural physics and structural science in general underpin the study of a range of technologically-important "functional" materials. The choice of the meeting topic and the title have been informed by current developments within both EPSRC and the Institute of Physics. Within EPSRC, there is an initiative to promote Crystallography or Structural Science as a discipline in its own right and, to reinforce this, our speakers have been drawn from Departments of Physics, Chemistry and Materials. Within the Institute of Physics, we are going through the process of changing our identity from the Physical Crystallography Group to the Structural Physics group in a bid to reflect more accurately the activities of a wide number of our members. The topic "Structural Physics of Condensed Matter" now appears as an EPSRC/IOP heading.

Please note that we have invited both EPSRC and IOP representatives to this meeting and we urge you to give it your strongest support.


1998 Autumn Meeting of the Physical Crystallography Group of the BCA

The Autumn Meeting of the PCG, entitled

'The Structural Physics of Functional Materials'

will take place at the University of Warwick (Radcliffe House)

The anticipated cost will be £25 for full members and £18 for student/retired members. This will include a carvery-style restaurant lunch, morning tea and afternoon coffee. The meeting will commence at around 10.30 a.m. and end at around 4.30 p.m. Further details of the meeting, including the registration procedure, will be posted on the BCA World-Wide Web pages as soon as they are available (around July 1st). They will also appear in the next issue of Crystallography News and will be sent via the Institute of Physics direct- mailing to IOP group members.
