Autumn Meeting 2003

BCA Physical Crystallography Group
and the
IoP Structural Condensed Matter Physics Group

Probing Structure at the Nanoscale:- Fact, Fiction or Hype?
Cosener's House, Abingdon, OXON, 8-9 December 2003

Hosted at Cosener's House, Abingdon, OXON and supported by the Physical Crystallography Group and the Centre for Materials Physics & Chemistry, CCLRC.

Organised by Dr J. C. Wasse, University College London and Prof P. Radaelli ISIS Facility, RAL

This meeting will focus upon the theme of disorder in materials and will cover a wide range of current studies. In particular we would like the audience to learn about the rersearch of disordered materials making a sweep from the liquid state to disorder in single crystals, but not forgetting magnetic disorder. The philosophy of the meeting concerns the range of approaches and techniques used in studying the "disordered" systems of interest, in particular the development of analytical techniques used in extracting the scientific information. The level of the presentations should appeal to beginners and experts alike.

We encourage graduate students to attend, and to bring posters for display at the meeting.

Confirmed speakers include:

Daniel Bowron (ISIS)
Studies of Liquids using complementary Neutron and X-ray Scattering techniques
Robert McGreevy (ISIS)
Magnetic Disorder in Crystalline Materials
Trevor Rayment (Cambridge)
Using X-ray Absorption Spectroscopy techniques to probe the Electrochemical Interface
Martin Dove (Cambridge)
Complementary Experimental and Theoretical techniques to understand Local Structure, Phase Transitions and Dynamic Structural Distortions in Materials
Jens Kreisel (ENSPG, Grenoble, France)
Single Crystal Diffuse Scattering techniques
Simon Billinge (Michigan State University, USA)
The advancement of Pair Distribution Function techniques to obtain Structural information of Complex Materials
