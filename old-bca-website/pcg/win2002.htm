BCA Physical Crystallography Group
and the
IoP Structural Condensed Matter Physics Group

Winter Meeting 2002
11th December 2002

Complementary Techniques Applied to Crystallography
at
The Centre for Science at Extreme Conditions, University of Edinburgh

Organised by Dr D. R. Allan and sponsored by the School of Physics

The Autumn 2002 one-day meeting of the Physical Crystallography Group will cover the wide-ranging applications of complementary techniques to crystallography: i.e. techniques that provide a wealth of additional information on the structure and properties of crystalline materials that are inaccessible with conventional X-ray and neutron diffraction techniques. The meeting will include scientific presentations, accounts of the latest technical advances and the opportunity to view some of the instruments used for complementary methods within The College of Science and Engineering at The University of Edinburgh.

The meeting is FREE to members of the Physical Crystallography Group and a small registration fee will be charged to non members at the following rates: £15 for regular participants; £12 for members of the Institute of Physics; and £7.50 for students, unemployed or retired participants. Registration will include a FREE one-year membership of the PCG (with all its accompanying wide and extravagant benefits). The PCG will provide limited support to students for travel costs.

The schedule of the meeting is:-

10:00 - 11:00 Registration and Coffee
11:00 - 13:00 Tour of Facilities within the College of Science and Engineering
13:00 - 14:00 Lunch sponsored by The School of Physics
14:00 - 14:30 S.J. Clark, Department of Physics, The University of Durham
First Principles Methods for Calculating the Structure and Electronic Properties of Materials
14:30 - 15:00 P. McMillan, Department of Chemistry, University College London
Vibrational spectroscopy as a complement to Diffraction Studies
15:00 - 15:30 M. Smith, Department of Physics, The University of Warwick
Applications of Solid-State NMR to Characterising Crystalline Materials
15:30 - 15:45 Coffee
15:45 - 16:15 A.M. Glazer, Department of Physics, The University of Oxford
Modern Optical Birefringence Studies of Crystals
16:15 - 16:45 I.M. Reaney, Department of Engineering Materials, The University of Sheffield
Electron Diffraction Studies of Simple Perovskites
16:45 - 17:15 S.J. Blundell, Clarendon Laboratory, The University of Oxford
Spin-Polarized Muons as a Probe of Local Magnetism
17:15 - 17:30 Discussion and closing remarks

For more information on the meeting contact the organiser: Dr David Allan (The University of Edinburgh, D.R.Allan@ed.ac.uk) or the PCG chairman: Dr Pam Thomas (The University of Warwick, P.A.Thomas@warwick.ac.uk).
