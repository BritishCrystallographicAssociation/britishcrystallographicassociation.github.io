BCA Physical Crystallography Group
and the
IoP Structural Condensed Matter Physics Group

Winter Meeting 2003
8-9th December 2003

Probing Structure at the Nanoscale:- Fact, Fiction or Hype?
hosted at
Cosener's House, Abingdon, Oxon, 8-9 December 2003

and supported by
the Physical Crystallography Group and the Centre for Materials Physics & Chemistry, CCLRC

Organised by Dr J. C. Wasse, University College London and Prof P. Radaelli ISIS Facility, RAL

The Winter 2003 meeting of the Physical Crystallography Group will focus upon the theme of disorder in materials and will cover a wide range of current studies. In particular we would like the audience to learn about the rersearch of disordered materials making a sweep from the liquid state to disorder in single crystals, but not forgetting magnetic disorder. The philosophy of the meeting concerns the range of approaches and techniques used in studying the "disordered" systems of interest, in particular the development of analytical techniques used in extracting the scientific information. The level of the presentations should appeal to beginners and experts alike.

The meeting will start with lunch at Cosener's House from 12:30 on Monday 8 December, followed by the introduction at 13:50. Monday's proceedings will end at about 18:00 in plenty of time for the evening meal. We will resume after breakfast on Tuesday morning, aiming to end with lunch at around 12:30. Over the 2 days, there will be 6 talks of about 45 mins. in length, with up to 4 of about 20 mins. in length, as well as poster presentations.

Please click here to find out details about Cosener's House, and the Travel Inn at Abingdon.

We encourage graduate students to attend, and to bring posters for display at the meeting.

Confirmed speakers include:

Daniel Bowron (ISIS)
Studies of Liquids using complementary Neutron and X-ray Scattering techniques
Robert McGreevy (ISIS)
Magnetic Disorder in Crystalline Materials
Trevor Rayment (Cambridge)
Using X-ray Absorption Spectroscopy techniques to probe the Electrochemical Interface
Martin Dove (Cambridge)
Complementary Experimental and Theoretical techniques to understand Local Structure, Phase Transitions and Dynamic Structural Distortions in Materials
Jens Kreisel (ENSPG, Grenoble, France)
Diffuse X-ray and Raman Scattering of "Nano-Structured" Oxides
Simon Billinge (Michigan State University, USA)
The advancement of Pair Distribution Function techniques to obtain Structural information of Complex Materials
