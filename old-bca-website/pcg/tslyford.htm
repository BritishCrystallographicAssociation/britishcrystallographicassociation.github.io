Report on ECM 19 Conference, Nancy, France
by
T. S. Lyford.

September 7th, 2000

Below I have given a brief description of the sessions I found most useful at the conference.

1. Session o.m1.o Ferroelectricity and NLO materials. The most interesting and informative talk I attended in this session was one given by F. Laurell, titled "Micro-structured ferroelectrics for advanced optical applications". His talk covered the history of periodically domain- inverted structures, their manufacture, applications and future work, which related directly to my Ph.D. project. Having studied his predecessors' work it was very interesting to see how certain techniques and ideas have advanced.
2. Session o.m2.o Perovskites: Materials of the 21st century. In the Crystallography Group at Warwick University, of which I am a member, principal research is into A-site perovskite substituted structures. This session was very interesting and I was able to discuss many of the topics raised with other members of the group.
3. Session s7'.m1.o Neutron Diffraction. My project does not involve any neutron diffraction and my knowledge in this field is minimal, but this session provided a very interesting insight into the underlying principles as well as current research interests. I have to admit that several of the more in-depth talks went over my head but I feel a lot more confident approaching the subject now.
4. Session o.m11.o Teaching Crystallography. I found this session interesting especially the talk given by A. M. Glazer, titled "Teaching crystallography to reluctant physicists". A lot of fundamental principles were presented in a basic yet amusing manner. Having only recently finished my degree, which contained only a small amount of crystallography, it was interesting to see how another University, Oxford, would have taught this subject.
