PCG membership in 1997

Important notice to members of the BCA PCG

joining via the Institute of Physics

In past years, it has been possible to join the BCA via the Physical Crystallography Group of the Institute of Physics. However, the joining fee for the Institute of Physics group is only £3 whereas the cost of BCA membership is £12, which covers an annual charge of £10 for the four issues of Crystallography News. It is obvious that the £3 group joining fee does not cover the cost of BCA membership. The financial shortfall has recently been recognized by both the BCA and the IOP. Therefore a change of arrangement has to be made to reflect the fact that the cost of being a full member of the BCA is £12.

After discussions with the Physical Crystallography Group Committee and BCA Council, the following has been decided for the calendar year 1997. Membership of the Physical Crystallography Group of the IOP will no longer carry with it full BCA membership. In order to obtain full BCA membership, providing with it four issues of Crystallography News per year, IOP group members will have to pay the difference between the group fee and the BCA membership fee i.e. £9.00, directly to the BCA on completion of the membership form which accompanies the December 96 issue of Crystallography News. If the balance is not paid, then Crystallography News will cease to be sent to you in 1997. However, you will retain low-cost registration at BCA events and will have access to news about the BCA and Physical Crystallography Group via the BCA World-wide Web pages.

We apologize for the inconvenience that this may cause to members, particularly those who have only just returned to the BCA lists after the Group Secretary's recruitment drive earlier this year. We hope that all of you will continue to opt for full BCA membership and elect to pay the £9.00 balance to the BCA for this.
Pam Thomas
Hon-Sec of the Physical Crystallography Group of the Institute of Physics
