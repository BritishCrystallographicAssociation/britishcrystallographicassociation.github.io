<h1>MEMBERSHIP of the BCA</h1>
<h3>Benefits of individual membership</h3>
Here is a list of benefits of belonging to the BCA:
<ol>
 <li><strong><em>THE</em></strong> professional organisation for=20
crystallographers in the UK, operated by crystallographers for=20
crystallographers
 <li>Comprehensive schedule of UK meetings, including the Annual BCA =
Meeting
each Spring, plus one-day meetings and workshops organised by subject =
groups
 <li>Reduced rates for all meetings organised under BCA auspices
 <li>Regular listings of other meetings of crystallographic interest,
both in the UK and worldwide
 <li>After 6 months of membership, students and postdocs can apply for=20
BCA Bursary awards for attendance at any relevant scientific meeting
 <li>Receive the free 64-page BCA Newsletter in A4 format every
quarter,  full of crystallographic news and information, product
advertisements, meeting schedules, job listings, etc.

 <li>Have your say in the development of crystallography, and nominate =
and=20
vote for BCA Officers, Council Members and subject-group Committee =
Members -=20
or stand for office yourself

 <li>Current subscription covers UK membership of the European
Crystallographic Association and over half the UK subscription to IUCr
 </ol>

<h3>Membership Subscription</h3>
Ordinary subscriptions for membership are &#163; 15 (pounds sterling) =
annually,
due on 1st January each year. Further information from the Treasurer is =
on=20
<a href=3D"../CNews/1998/Jun98/Pages.html#p3">
page 3 of the June 98</a> issue of 'Crystallography News'.<p>

Discounts are available to Students, Retired members, and those who pay =
by
Standing Order. There are also special arrangements for Overseas =
Members.<P>

Click here
for <a href=3D"mform02.html">details from 1 Jan 2002</a>
or write for a membership form to our <a href=3D"#admin">Administration =
office
.</a><Br>

<a name=3D"DatProt">Members</a> names, addresses and details of their =
choice of=20
BCA Groups and date of
payment of subscriptions are held in a computer database, to simplify =
our
membership records and for the mailing of 'Crystallography News'.

Members can check details of their entry in the BCA database at any time =

by contacting the Administration office, or interactively at the Annual =
Spring=20
meeting.<p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal><b><span style=3D'font-size:12.0pt'>ARNOLD BEEVERS =
BURSARIES
IN 2002</span></b></p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>&nbsp;</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>The bursary fund was renamed the Arnold Beevers =
Bursary Fund
in April 2001 and endowed with =A320,000 from the funds received from =
Crystal
Congress Glasgow. BCA Council gratefully acknowledges several generous
donations to the fund in its first few months of operation and welcomes =
further
contributions to this worthwhile cause.</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal><b>GIFT AID: </b>The funds have also been boosted =
by a
council decision to allocate Gift Aid refunds to the Arnold Beevers =
Bursary
Fund. The 2001 Gift Aid refund of=A0 =A3668.32 has added to the fund and =
council
thanks the 183 members who have signed a gift aid declaration and =
contributed
to the claim. Members who haven't already done so and are eligible for =
the
scheme are encouraged to sign a Gift Aid declaration. Your membership =
renewal
documents for 2002 will advise you if you have already signed a =
form.</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>&nbsp;</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal><b>Applying for Bursaries in 2002</b></p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal><b>&nbsp;</b></p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal><b>Bursaries for Nottingham Spring Meeting (25- 28 =
March
2002):</b></p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>A limited number of bursaries are available from =
the Arnold
Beevers Bursary Fund to cover the =A3165 three day accommodation package =
and =A315
student conference dinner at Nottingham (Student Registration is Free). =
The
bursary will not cover travel expenses and recipients will be expected =
to
present a poster.</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>Council is seeking commercial sponsors of Spring =
Meeting
Bursaries and it is hoped to offer some Named Bursaries at this =
meeting.</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>The <b>closing date</b> for all applications is =
<b>25th
January 2002</b>, applicants should ask for an Arnold Beevers bursary
application form from the BCA Administrative Office (details below).</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>&nbsp;</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal><b>Bursaries for other BCA Meetings and =
Schools:</b></p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>These are administered directly by the organisers =
of the
Meeting or Teaching School. They will publicise the procedure for their =
bursary
applications, whether funded by the BCA itself or a constituent =
group.</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>&nbsp;</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal><b>General Bursaries:</b></p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>Arnold Beevers Bursaries are available to members, =
(see
point (a) below) who wish to attend relevant scientific meetings in the =
UK or
abroad which are not organised by the BCA.</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>&nbsp;</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>All applications are considered by the BCA Bursary
Committee, comprising the President, Vice President, Treasurer and =
Secretary
and are guided by the following considerations: </p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>&nbsp;</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>(a) The bursaries are primarily intended to support =
bona
fide research students and postdoctoral workers who are BCA members.
Applications from those with more senior non-tenured positions, and from =
those
in junior permanent positions where university or industrial funds are
unavailable, may be supported at the discretion of the BCA Bursary =
Committee if
BCA funds are available. </p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>(b) Applicants must normally have been a BCA member =
for 6
months before applying. </p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>(c) Applicants will not normally be considered if =
they have
been awarded a general bursary in the previous two years. </p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>(d) Preference will be given to applicants who have =
attended
a BCA meeting. Applications must be received at least 6 weeks before the =
date
of the meeting/visit. No retrospective applications are possible. </p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal><b>&nbsp;</b></p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>&nbsp;</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>Successful applicants are expected to submit a
word-processed short scientific report on the meeting/visit soon after =
their
return; this should include details of the sessions attended and of the =
applicant's
presentation. The reports may be submitted via electronic mail to the <a
href=3D"../My%20Documents/Dell/BCA/My%20Documents/Dell/BCA/Treasurer/memb=
er.html#admin"><span
style=3D'color:windowtext;text-decoration:none'>=A0BCA Administrative =
Office </span></a>=A0and
may be published later in '<em>Crystallography News</em>'. </p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal>&nbsp;</p>

<p class=3DMsoNormal><b>Applicants should ask for an Arnold Beevers =
bursary application
form from the BCA Administrative Office</b></p>
