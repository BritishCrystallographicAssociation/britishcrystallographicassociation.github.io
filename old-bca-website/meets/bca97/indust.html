Industrial Crystallography

The Alun Bowen Industrial Lecture

This will be delivered by Prof. Paul Barnes and will be entitled: "Cine-crystallography Plus"

Session 1: Diffraction Plus.... Simultaneous Methods
Tuesday afternoon - 15 April 1997

Provisional Programme

Speakers arranged include:
    Dr Helen Gleeson (Department of Physics, The University of Manchester)
      "Simultaneous x-ray and Raman scattering studies of liquid crystals"
    Dr Ian Hamley (School of Chemistry, The University of Leeds)
      "Correlating material properties of polymer with structure: a new rheometer for x-ray scattering studies"
    Dr Tony Ryan (UMIST Materials Science Centre)
      "SAXS and WAXS during polymer fibre spinning"
    Dr Richard van Gelder (Chemistry Department, Strathclyde University)
      "X-ray Diffraction Studies of Surfactant Crystallisation" (provisional title)

Further offers of oral and poster contributions will be welcomed and should be addressed to the organiser - Dr. C.D. Adam (Email: sctcda@staffs.ac.uk, Tel: +44 (0)1782 294782).

Wednesday morning - 16 April 1996
Process & Production Applications

This session aims to take in a range of activity from on-line measurement (taking the technique to the process) through to laboratory study under simulated process conditions, with off-line production analysis on the way.

Provisional Programme

Speakers provisionally arranged include:
    Mr Reinhard Beilman (Hermann Milke, Germany)
      "Automated XRD analysis in cement production"
    Mrs Mary Halliwell (Philips Analytical X-Ray, Almelo, Netherlands) and Rodney Moss (Hewlett Packard)
      "The role of XRD in optoelectronic device production"
    Mr Colin Small (Rolls-Royce)
      "Texture in titanium for fan blade manufacture"
    Dr Christopher Frampton (Hoffmann-LaRoche)
      "Polymorphism and phase analysis in relation to pharmaceutical development"

Further offers of contributions and enquiries should be addressed to the session organiser Dr. S.V. Norval (Email: steve_norval@ici.co.uk, Tel: +44 (0)1642 432005, Fax: +44 (0)1642 437490).
