LECTURES and POSTERS

ANNUAL SPRING MEETING

UNIVERSITY OF LEEDS, 14 - 17 APRIL 1997

      INSTRUCTIONS

Poster contributions are invited on any aspect of crystallography. They are an essential part of a successful meeting. A good poster has both scientific content and clear presentation. Points to remember: What are you trying to say? Is it readable from a few feet away? Is it eye-catching? Is there too much text or is t in too small a size? A Velcro-covered board 4' wide x 3' high will be provided for each accepted poster it is strongly recommended to mount your poster on this of backing sheet. There are prizes for the best poster in each group.

Poster Abstracts may be submitted by e-mail or as camera-ready copy. E-mail is preferred - submission details are summarized here. Camera-ready copies should be printed in black on a single A4 sheet. Side margins should be at least 3cm, top and bottom margins should be at least 4cm, and the font size should 12 point. The title should be in CAPITALS, followed by the author(s) name(s) and affiliations. Underline those authors who will be at the meeting as in the example:

    KOSSEL PATTERNS OBTAINED FROM QUASICRYSTALS

    Ch. Schetelich1, S. Brennner2 and V. Geist2

    1    Physics Department, University of Warwick, 2Institute of Mineralogy, Crystallography and Material Science University of Leipzig.

Please send the original and one good copy to the BCA abstracts organiser, indicating the session for which it is intended (BSG, CCG, IG, or PCG) now or certainly before 1 March 1997.

Lecturers are reminded that they should submit an abstract of their lecture following the same guidelines and deadline as those for the poster presentations.
