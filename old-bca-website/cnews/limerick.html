June Competition Winner

Write a Limerick with a Crystallographic theme, similar to the example below

There was a young lady from Bristol
Who grew a most beautiful crystal
Its structure she found
Was cubic not round
That smart little lady from Bristol

 The winner was

Ann Chippindale, Chemical Crystallography Lab, OXFORD

Here are her winning entries:

An X-ray scatterer from Cheam,
Grew crystals that went off in the beam,
"Curse it", he said,
"I'll use neutrons instead",
And the experiment worked like a dream.

A young crystallographer from Hong Kong
Got his Spacegroup completly wrong
He ran out of luck
When the refinement got stuck
With bond distances too short and too long
