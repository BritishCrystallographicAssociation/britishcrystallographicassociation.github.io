Books and reviews

  • 'X-ray Diffrraction at Elevated Temperatures. A method for in-situ Process Analysis'
    ( D.D.L.Chung, P.W.De Haven, H.Arnold, D. Ghosh)
    ISBN 0-89573-745-0 or ISBN 3-527-27842-7 (1992)

  • ‘Applied Crystallography Solutions to Problems in Industrial Solid State Chemistry: Case examples with ceramics, cements and Zeolites'
    ( P.Barnes, X.Turrillas, A.C.Jupe, S.L.Colston, D.O'Connor, R.J.Cernik, P.Livesey, C.Hall, D.Bates, and R.Dennis)
    J. Chem. Soc. Faraday Trans.92(12) (1996) p 2187

    This is a recent compendium of several aspaects of work on in-situ diffraction, particularly the combining of different techniques.

  • ‘Time-resolved diffraction'
    ( Edited by J.R. Helliwell and P.M. Rentzepis)
    Oxford University Press (1997)

  • 'Crystallography Across the Sciences'
    ed H.Schenk,
    IUCr ISBN 87-16-16327-3 a review volume reprinted from Section 1 Acta Cryst A54 Nov 98 celebrating 50 years of Act Cryst and the IUCr, contains 26 papers across a wide area of crystallography, well worth reading. (see also individual paper listed below)

Research papers on applications of 'in situ' diffraction

Papers listed in chronological order

  • ‘A neutron diffraction investigation of the LaNi5 - D phase diagram'
    (EH Kisi, EM Gray and SJ Kennedy)
    J Alloys Compounds, 216 (1994) p 123

  • ‘Aging of Mg-PSZ'
    (DN Argyriou, CJ Howard and RI Smith)
    J Amer. Ceram. Soc., 77 (1994) p 3073

  • ‘Synchrotron-related Studies on the Dynamic and Structural aspects of Zirconia Synthesis for Ceramic and Catalytic Applications'
    ( X Turrillas, P Barnes, D Gascoigne, JZ Turner, SL Jones, CJ Norman, CF Pygall and AJ Dent)
    Radiation Phys.& Chem. 45 (1995) p 491

    A High temperature in-situ study of zirconia synthesis using neutrons, synchrotron diffraction and EXAFS

  • ‘Structure of deuterated ammonia-IV'
    ( JS Loveday, RJ Nelmes, WG Marshall, JM Besson, S Klotz and G Hamel)
    Phys. Rev. Lett. 76 (1996) p 74

  • ‘Structural instability in Ice VIII under pressure'
    (JM Besson, S Klotz, G Hamel, WG Marshall, RJ Nelmes and JS Loveday)
    Phys. Rev. Lett. 78 (1996) p L597

  • ‘High temperature study of octahedral cation exchange in olivine by neutron powder diffraction'
    (CMB Henderson, KS Knight, SAT Redfern and BJ Wood)
    Science 271 (1996) p 1713

  • ‘Fast in-situ X-ray diffraction studies of chemical reactions: a synchrotron view of the hydration of tricalcium aluminate'
    ( AC Jupe, X Turrillas, P Barnes, SL Colston, C Hall, D Hdusermann, and M Hanfland)
    Phys. Rev. B (Rapid Commun.) 53 (1996) R14697

    This cement hydration reaction requires the presence of an intermediate on the timescales of seconds. Time-resolved powder diffraction patterns were collected in time frames down to 300 milliseconds)

  • ‘Determination of olivine cooling rates from metal-cation ordering'
    (SAT Redfern, CMB Henserson, BJ Wood, RJ Harrison and KS Knight)
    Nature 381 (1996) p 407

  • ‘Cation ordering in olivines): Recrystallisation of melt cast BiSCCO samples'
    ( PS Whitfield, JR Bhakta, RI Smith, I Gameson, R Cywinski and JS Abell)
    Rev. Sci. Inst. 69 (1998) p 2475

    Recrystallisation of glassy rods by passing DC current through rod under controlled atmospheres while viewing the sample with a video camera.

  • ‘In-situ Hydration studies using Multi-angle Energy-Dispersive Diffraction'
    (SL Colston, SDM Jacques, P Barnes, AC Jupe, and C Hall)
    J. Syncr. Rad. 5, (1998) p 112

    Novel method of in-situ energy dispersive diffraction in which three detectors are used to gain a more complete coverage of reciprocal space. The method is illustrated with results from cement hydration studies.

  • ‘Pressure dependence of hydrogen bonding in metal deuteroxides: a neutron powder diffraction study of Mn(OD)2 and beta-Co(OD)2'
    (JB Parise, B Theroux, R Li, JS Loveday and WG Marshall)
    Phys. Chem. Min. 25 (1998) p 130

  • Real Time 'in situ' observation of chemical reactions
    (Yuji Ohashi)
    Section 1 Acta Cryst(1998) A54 November p842-849
