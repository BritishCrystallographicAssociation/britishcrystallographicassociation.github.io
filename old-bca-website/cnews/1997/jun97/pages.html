President's Remarks

From the President.

Many thanks to Mark Thornton-Pett and his team for the excellent Spring Meeting that many of us have just enjoyed in Leeds. The scientific content was of a high standard and at the same time entertaining, and the conference facilities were second to none. As President I was conscious of having to be everywhere at the same time, with one committee meeting after another: nevertheless I did get to many of the sessions and activities and thoroughly enjoyed it all. Mind you, by the time the weekend came I crashed out! Well, next year it will be Phil Lightfoot's turn to organise our meeting, this time at the University of St. Andrews. I know it is a long way to go, but I hope you will make the effort to attend. I believe that there are other attractions in the area besides crystallography.

Note that there will not be a BCA meeting in 1999, as that will be the year of the IUCr Congress in Glasgow.

At the banquet in Leeds, I pointed out that it will shortly be the 90th birthday of one of our oldest (if not the oldest) members, Dr. Helen Megaw, and we arranged for everyone to sign a greeting card to her. I hope to get it to her in Northern Ireland, where she retired, in due course. In the meantime I would like to wish her a Happy Birthday from myself and from the BCA.
Mike Glazer
Oxford
